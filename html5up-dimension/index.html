About Me

I'm a born-and-raised Californian originally from the Monterey Bay. I started my academic career as a fifth-generation student at UC Berkeley. I initially wanted to study Psychology, but fell in love with Linguistics after taking an introductory course for my Psychology degree. I decided to have my cake and eat it too and finished college on the day of my 21st birthday with a double major. As an undergraduate, I was involved in many interdisciplinary labs, which you can read about in my CV.

Now I work as a researcher at UCSF in the Chang Lab where I keep busy giving a physiological reality to my love for linguistics. You can read more about my research interests here.

When I'm not immersed in brains or phonemes (or both, ideally), I spend my free time backpacking, playing Magic: The Gathering, and managing my record collection.

My Research

"It's so easy for a kid to join a gang, to do drugs ... we should make it that easy to be involved in football and academics." ~ Snoop Dogg

How do we acquire language, how do we store it, and how do we adapt it? These are the questions that intrigue me as a researcher. My clinical experiences at UCSF have inspired me to discover as much as I can about how language manifests itself in our neural architecture: even when presented with the adversity of a large temporal lobe tumor, language can reorganize around the malignant tissue, proving it is one slippery beast to pin down. The sheer volume of biological resources we devote to acquiring this social tool proves its importance. Yet there is more to be learned, and nothing is more exciting to me.

I plan on pursuing a doctorate studying the neural plasticity of language, whether it be in infants (the best linguists in the world) or in those unfortunate enough to have their language impaired by a tumor or traumatic brian injury. I am currently looking at different programs and hope to start my studies in Fall 2018.

Research Interests

Research Experience

  • UCSF Department of Neurological Surgery (2016-present). Research specialist studying aphasia found in postoperative neurosurgical patients. PI: Edward F. Chang, MD.
  • UC Berkeley Department of Linguistics (2015). Undergraduate researcher studying coarticulation and individual variation of coda /r/ and /l/ using ultrasound vocal tract imaging. PI: Sarah Bakst.
  • UC Berkeley Department of Linguistics (2015). Undergraduate researcher documenting and morphologically glossing Matsigenka, a highly polysynthetic Arawakan language. PI: Lev Michael, PhD.
  • Golden Bear Sleep & Mood Research Clinic (2014-2015). Research assistant studying cognitive functioning in teenagers with poor sleep hygiene. PI: Allison Harvey, PhD.

Teaching Experience

  • UC Berkeley DeCal Program (2013-2015). Student instructor of two unit "Intro to 3D Modeling and Printing" class. Co-taught class on fundamentals of 3D modeling and 3D printing in Autodesk CAD software with three other student instructors for three semesters.

Academic and Community Service

  • Society for Linguistics Undergraduate Students (SLUgS) (2014-2015). Served as an officer for Berkeley's undergraduate linguistics club. Created discussion about undergraduate and graduate research on campus and invited many high-profile linguists to give talks for interested undergrads.
  • 3D Modeling Club (3DMC) (2013-2015). Helped found UC Berkeley's first 3D modeling and printing club, which now has its own student-taught course, Makerspace, and figurative armada of 3D printers.
  • FIRST Lego League (2012). Mentored students at Carmel Middle School in designing and building robots for a FIRST-affiliated robotics competition.
